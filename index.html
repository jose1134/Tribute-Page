A Tribute to

Stan Lee

The Man behind many of the greatest stories and comic heroes

How the great man came to be:

  • 1922: The birth of Stanley Martin Lieber in New York City, N.Y.
  • 1938: Lieber attended DeWitt Clinton High School with the goal of becoming a writer
  • 1939: Started on his first comic book ‘Captain America Foils the Traitor's Revenge’
  • 1941: he started getting opportunites to work on comics, starting with ‘Destroyer in Mystic Comics No. 6’, ‘Jack Frost in USA Comic No. 1’ and ‘Father Time in Captain America Comics No.6’
  • 1942: he joined the United States Army and served stateside in the Signal Corps
  • 1947: Stan married Jan Clayton Boocock. He was 24 years old, turning 25 in less than 30 days.
  • 1950: Joan and Stan had their first child, a baby girl they named Joan Celia, who later took the nickname, J.C.
  • Early 1950s: after the army, he got back into the comic book buisness ad join 'Atlas Comics'
  • 1950s: after joining Atlas, he started experiment on different genres such as writing romantic, sci-fi, horror, humorous stories, etc.
  • Late 1950s: in competition with DC Comics, Lee’s publisher, Goodman, asked him to create a new superhero team. Lee thought to work on the stories that he believed in since he was getting disgruntled with his monotonous career.-For this assignment, Lee, in association with his colleague Jack Kirby, created a team of superheroes called ‘Fantastic Four’ with superheroes like, ‘Hulk’, ‘Iron Man’, ‘Thor’, ‘Spider-Man, ‘X-Men’, ‘Doctor Strange’, etc.
  • 1953: Sadly, Stan had a misfortunate turn in his life. Losing his second daughter Jan, who died a few days after her birth in 1953.
  • 1960s: He scripted, art-directed and edited most of Marvel's series while moderating the letters pages. He was also writing a monthly column, ‘Stan’s Soapbox’. His job was getting very taxing but Lee, for once, was enjoying it.
  • 1971: Lee was asked to write a story on bad-effects of drugs and he thought of including it in ‘The Amazing Spider-Man’...Lee and Goodman went ahead with it anyway and published the story in their comics. The story got really famous and Marvel was appreciated for spreading the responsible message. The CCA abolished the Code and permitted negative depictions of drugs.
  • 1975: He was more busy and gained popularity as a figurehead and public face for Marvel Comics. He attended the comic book conventions around America, lectured at colleges and participated in panel discussions.
  • 1981: Lee’s role in the company was becoming bigger and bigger, he was given the role to develop Marvel’s TV and movie properties, for which he had to shift to California with his family.
  • 2000: He got involved with the DC Comics for the first time in his creative career, when he launched ‘Just Imagine…’ series for them, in which he re-created DC superheroes Like, Superman, Batman, Wonder Woman, etc.
  • 2006: When Lee finished his 65 years with Marvel, it honored him with the publication of a series of one-shot comics in which Lee was shown interacting with his co-creations like ‘Spider-Man’, ‘Doctor Strange’, etc.
  • 2007: at the Comic-Con International, a Stan Lee action figure was launched in order to honor him. The body used in the making of action figure was a re-used mold of Spider-Man figure with some minor changes.
  • 2008-present: Marvel studios relased a string of comic book movies, mostly from the comics of the great Stan Lee
  • 2018: Lee died at the age of 95 at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center in Los Angeles, California, on November 12, 2018, after being rushed there in a medical emergency earlier in the day. Earlier that year, Lee revealed to the public that he had been battling pneumonia and in February was rushed to the hospital for worsening conditions at around the same time

"You know, my motto is 'Excelsior.' That's an old word that means 'upward and onward to greater glory.' It's on the seal of the state of New York. Keep moving forward, and if it's time to go, it's time. Nothing lasts forever." -Stan Lee
